Behind the beer

Designing the Motion Series

The spirit of everyday motion in a beer.

The idea behind the Motion Series was to create a lineup of beers that capture the feeling of being in motion—whether that’s hiking, biking, fishing, or just moving through the day. Each beer is designed to evoke a different aspect of that experience, from the slow, rhythmic pace of a calm morning to the energetic rush of an afternoon adventure. The goal was to create a cohesive family of beers that feel connected but also have their own distinct personalities and flavor profiles.

The design process was all about finding the right balance between simplicity and storytelling. We wanted the labels to be visually striking and immediately recognizable, but also to convey a sense of place and mood. The monkeys became a central part of that story—they’re not just mascots, they’re characters that embody the spirit of each beer and the overall vibe of the brand.

Brew Log #001 — El Pescador Pils

A crisp, clean pilsner built for early mornings, calm water, and the first cast of the day.

Style: Pilsner (Lager)
Series: Motion
ABV: ~5.0%
IBU: ~25
Status: v1 concept

The idea
El Pescador Pils was one of the first beers that felt like it truly belonged in the lowcase lineup. It’s built around the rhythm of the Valley—early mornings, still water, and the kind of patience that comes with fishing before the day really starts.
The build
This is a traditional pilsner approach: clean fermentation, restrained hopping, and a focus on clarity and balance. Nothing flashy, nothing overworked—just a beer that does exactly what it’s supposed to do.
Flavor direction
Light malt backbone with a subtle herbal hop character and a crisp, dry finish. The goal is maximum drinkability—something you can sip slowly or crush without thinking too much about it.
Visual direction
The label leans into sunrise tones and coastal calm—soft gold light reflecting off the water, a quiet harbor scene, and a monkey posted up fishing. It’s not about motion here, it’s about focus.
Where it fits
This is technically part of the Motion series, but it sits at the slowest end of that spectrum. It’s not about speed—it’s about rhythm, patience, and being present in the moment.
